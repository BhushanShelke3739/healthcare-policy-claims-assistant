Title: Out-of-Network Claim Policy
Plan: HealthFirst Cooperative (Demo)
Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Document Owner: Network Management

1. Purpose

This policy describes how Out-of-Network (OON) claims are reviewed and
paid, when OON cost-sharing applies, when in-network protections apply
under the federal No Surprises Act, and the provider submission
requirements.

2. Definitions

  2.1 In-Network (INN) Provider — a provider holding a current
      participating provider agreement with HealthFirst.
  2.2 Out-of-Network (OON) Provider — a provider without such an
      agreement.
  2.3 Allowed Amount — the maximum amount HealthFirst pays for a covered
      service. For OON services it is set per the Allowed Amount Schedule
      (Section 5).
  2.4 Balance Bill — the difference between a provider's billed charge
      and the allowed amount, where the provider seeks to collect from
      the member.

3. Coverage of OON Services

OON services are covered under one of the following conditions:

  3.1 Emergency Services
      Emergency services are covered at the in-network cost-sharing
      level regardless of where they are rendered. The member is held
      harmless from balance billing for the emergency portion of care.

  3.2 No In-Network Provider Available
      If no in-network provider is geographically or clinically
      available within network adequacy standards, a Network Gap
      exception is authorized in advance. Member cost-sharing is
      adjusted to the in-network level.

  3.3 Continuity of Care
      For members in an active course of treatment with a provider whose
      network status terminates, continuity-of-care benefits apply for
      up to ninety (90) calendar days, at the in-network cost-sharing
      level, when the qualifying conditions are met.

  3.4 Prior-Authorized Exception
      If HealthFirst issues a written authorization for a specific OON
      service before the date of service, the authorization controls
      cost-sharing and balance-billing protections per its terms.

  3.5 Standard OON Benefits
      For all other OON services, OON cost-sharing applies and the member
      may be balance billed.

4. Member Cost-Sharing

  4.1 In-Network Tier — applies in scenarios 3.1–3.4 above.
  4.2 Out-of-Network Tier — applies in scenario 3.5.
       - Deductible (individual): $3,000.
       - Coinsurance after deductible: 40% (member responsibility).
       - Out-of-pocket maximum (individual): $15,000.

5. Allowed Amount Schedule

For non-emergency OON services not covered under a Surprise Billing
protection, the Allowed Amount is the lesser of:
  - The provider's billed charge, or
  - 150% of the current-year Medicare allowable for the geographic
    locality, or
  - The plan's published OON fee schedule, where applicable.

6. Surprise Billing Protections (No Surprises Act)

For services subject to the federal No Surprises Act protections, the
member's cost-sharing is calculated as if the provider were in-network
and the provider is prohibited from balance billing the member for the
difference. The Qualifying Payment Amount (QPA) is used to determine
the member's cost-sharing.

Protections apply to:
  - Emergency services rendered at any facility.
  - Non-emergency services delivered by OON providers at in-network
    facilities (e.g., anesthesiology, radiology, pathology), unless the
    member knowingly consented in writing per the federal notice and
    consent requirements.
  - Air ambulance services.

7. Provider Submission Requirements

OON providers must submit:
  - A complete CMS-1500 or UB-04 claim.
  - Their NPI and tax identification number.
  - The standard prior-authorization reference number, when applicable.
  - For Surprise Billing claims, the appropriate disclosure indicating
    No Surprises Act applicability.

Claims may be submitted on paper or electronically. Electronic
submission is encouraged for faster adjudication.

8. Timely Filing

OON claims must be received within one hundred and eighty (180) calendar
days of the date of service. Claims received after that window are
denied as Untimely Filing (HF-091) unless a documented good-cause
exception applies.

9. Appeal Rights

OON denials are appealable under the Appeal Process Policy on the same
timelines as in-network denials. Members continue to enjoy any
applicable Surprise Billing protections throughout the appeal.
