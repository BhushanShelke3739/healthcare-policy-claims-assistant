Title: HIPAA Minimum Necessary Standard Policy
Plan: HealthFirst Cooperative (Demo)
Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Document Owner: Privacy & Compliance Office

1. Purpose

This policy implements the HIPAA Privacy Rule "minimum necessary"
standard at HealthFirst Cooperative. The standard requires that
Protected Health Information (PHI) used, disclosed, or requested be
limited to the minimum amount reasonably necessary to accomplish the
intended purpose.

2. Scope

Applies to all workforce members, business associates, and downstream
contractors who handle PHI on behalf of HealthFirst Cooperative,
including claims adjudicators, customer service representatives, care
managers, and analytics staff.

3. Where the Minimum Necessary Standard Does Not Apply

The standard does NOT apply to the following uses or disclosures:
  3.1 Disclosures to or requests by a health care provider for
      treatment purposes.
  3.2 Disclosures to the individual who is the subject of the PHI.
  3.3 Uses or disclosures made pursuant to a valid HIPAA Authorization
      signed by the individual.
  3.4 Disclosures required by law (e.g., subpoena, court order).
  3.5 Uses or disclosures to the Department of Health and Human Services
      for compliance enforcement.
  3.6 Uses or disclosures required for compliance with the HIPAA
      regulations themselves.

4. Role-Based Access Tiers

Access to PHI is granted on the basis of job function. Tier
assignments are reviewed quarterly.

  Tier 1 — Limited Identifier Access
    Operations staff who require only member ID, plan ID, and benefit
    summary information.

  Tier 2 — Claim-Level Access
    Claims adjudicators and customer service representatives. Includes
    diagnosis codes, procedure codes, and EOB-equivalent data.

  Tier 3 — Clinical Record Access
    Utilization Management nurses, medical directors, and care managers.
    Includes chart notes and clinical records relevant to the case.

  Tier 4 — Investigative Access
    Special Investigations Unit (fraud, waste, and abuse). Access is
    case-scoped and time-bounded.

5. Routine Disclosures

For routine, recurring disclosures (e.g., monthly enrollment files to a
broker), a Minimum Necessary protocol is documented at the
disclosure-class level and reviewed annually. The protocol specifies the
data elements disclosed, the recipient, and the safeguards in place.

6. Non-Routine Requests

For non-routine requests, the Privacy Office performs an individualized
assessment of:
  6.1 Whether the requestor has authority to receive the PHI.
  6.2 The minimum data elements necessary to fulfill the request.
  6.3 Appropriate transmission safeguards (e.g., secure email,
      encrypted SFTP).

A non-routine request is approved or denied within ten (10) business
days. If denied, the requestor receives a written explanation and may
escalate to the Chief Privacy Officer within fifteen (15) business days.

7. De-Identification and Limited Data Sets

For analytics and research, PHI must be de-identified per 45 CFR
164.514(b) or transformed into a limited data set with a Data Use
Agreement on file before any release.

8. Audit and Monitoring

The Privacy Office audits PHI access on a sample basis monthly. All
workforce members must complete annual privacy training; failure to
complete training within thirty (30) days of the assigned deadline
results in temporary suspension of PHI access.

9. Sanctions

Violations of the Minimum Necessary Standard are addressed under the
Workforce Sanctions Policy. Sanctions range from re-training and
written warning to termination, depending on intent and impact.

10. Reporting

Suspected violations must be reported to the Privacy Office within
twenty-four (24) hours of discovery. The Privacy Office investigates,
documents findings, and notifies affected individuals if a reportable
breach is confirmed.
