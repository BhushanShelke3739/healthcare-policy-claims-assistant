1. Purpose

This policy describes the three-level appeal process available to
members and authorized representatives when a claim or prior
authorization request is denied. It sets the timeframes for filing
appeals, the timeframes for decisions, the documentation requirements,
and the option to request an external review.

2. Definitions

  2.1 Appeal — a member's (or authorized representative's) formal
      request to reconsider an adverse benefit determination.
  2.2 Adverse Benefit Determination — a denial, reduction, termination,
      or non-payment of a benefit, including a denial of prior
      authorization or a denial based on medical necessity.
  2.3 Expedited Appeal — an appeal for which the standard timeframe
      would seriously jeopardize the member's life, health, or ability
      to regain maximum function.

3. Levels of Appeal

  3.1 First-Level Appeal (Internal)
      - Filing window: within sixty (60) calendar days of the denial
        notice.
      - Decision window: within thirty (30) calendar days of receipt of
        a complete appeal for pre-service claims; within sixty (60)
        calendar days for post-service claims.
      - Reviewed by a qualified individual who was not involved in the
        original determination.

  3.2 Second-Level Appeal (Internal)
      - Filing window: within thirty (30) calendar days of the
        first-level decision notice.
      - Decision window: within thirty (30) calendar days of receipt.
      - Reviewed by a panel that includes a board-certified specialist
        in the same or similar specialty for medical-necessity appeals.

  3.3 External Review (Independent Review Organization)
      - Filing window: within sixty (60) calendar days of the
        second-level decision notice (or earlier if internal levels
        are exhausted).
      - Decision window: within forty-five (45) calendar days of the
        Independent Review Organization's (IRO) receipt of the request.
      - The IRO is contracted independent of HealthFirst and its
        decision is binding on the plan.

4. Expedited Appeals

For expedited appeals:
  4.1 May be filed by phone, in writing, or through the Member Portal.
  4.2 Decision must be issued within seventy-two (72) hours of receipt.
  4.3 External expedited review must be decided within seventy-two
      (72) hours by the IRO.

A request is treated as expedited if a treating physician certifies in
writing that the standard timeframe would seriously jeopardize the
member's life, health, or ability to regain maximum function.

5. How to File an Appeal

A complete appeal includes:
  - Member name, ID, and date of birth.
  - The claim number or PA reference number being appealed.
  - A copy of the denial notice.
  - A written statement of why the determination should be overturned.
  - Any supporting clinical records, peer-reviewed literature, or
    physician statements.

Appeals may be filed:
  - Through the Member Portal.
  - By mail to: HealthFirst Appeals, PO Box 0000, City, State 00000.
  - By secure fax to (555) 010-2345.

6. Acknowledgement

HealthFirst sends a written acknowledgement of every appeal within
five (5) business days of receipt.

7. Documentation Provided to the Member

Upon request and free of charge, HealthFirst provides:
  - Copies of all documents, records, and other information relevant
    to the claim.
  - The specific internal rule, guideline, or protocol relied upon.
  - The clinical criteria applied for medical-necessity determinations.

8. Concurrent Care

If the appeal concerns the termination or reduction of a previously
approved course of treatment, the existing course of treatment continues
during the appeal and the appeal is decided before the previously
approved service ends.

9. Recordkeeping

Appeal records, including the determination, the reviewer's credentials,
and the criteria applied, are retained for a minimum of ten (10) years.
