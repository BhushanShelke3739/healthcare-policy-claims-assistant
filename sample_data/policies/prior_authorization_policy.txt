Title: Prior Authorization Policy

Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Document Owner: Utilization Management

1. Purpose

This policy describes when Prior Authorization (PA) is required, how to
submit a request, decision timelines, and how to appeal a PA denial.
Prior Authorization is a determination that a covered service is
medically necessary and benefit-eligible before the service is provided.

2. Scope

Applies to all in-network and out-of-network providers serving members of
the HealthFirst Cooperative commercial and Medicare Advantage plans.

3. Services Requiring Prior Authorization

The following service categories require PA before they are rendered:
  3.1 Advanced imaging: MRI, CT, PET, and nuclear cardiology studies.
  3.2 Inpatient admissions (elective and scheduled).
  3.3 Outpatient surgeries performed in a hospital setting.
  3.4 Specialty injectable and infusion drugs on the formulary's PA list.
  3.5 Durable medical equipment with a billed amount exceeding $1,500.
  3.6 Home health services beyond 14 visits.
  3.7 Non-emergency air ambulance transport.

Emergency services and post-stabilization care never require PA.

4. Submission Channels

Providers must submit PA requests through one of:
  4.1 Provider Portal (preferred): real-time status updates.
  4.2 Secure fax to (555) 010-1234.
  4.3 Electronic Data Interchange (X12N 278 transaction).

Phone requests are accepted only for urgent / expedited reviews.

5. Required Information

A complete PA request includes:
  - Member ID, date of birth, and date of service (proposed).
  - Requesting provider NPI and servicing provider NPI.
  - CPT/HCPCS code(s) and ICD-10 diagnosis code(s).
  - Clinical justification with relevant chart notes.
  - Failed conservative treatments where applicable.

Incomplete requests are pended for additional information. The clinical
review clock pauses while the request is pended.

6. Decision Timelines

  6.1 Standard review: a determination is issued within five (5) business
      days of receipt of a complete request.
  6.2 Expedited / urgent review: a determination is issued within
      twenty-four (24) hours when delay would jeopardize the member's
      life, health, or ability to regain maximum function.
  6.3 Concurrent review (during an inpatient stay): a determination is
      issued within twenty-four (24) hours.

7. Communication of Decisions

Approval determinations are communicated to the requesting provider
electronically within one business day of the decision. Denial
determinations are communicated to both the provider and the member in
writing, and include:
  - The specific reason for denial.
  - The clinical criteria applied.
  - The member's right to appeal and the timeframe to file.
  - The reviewer's credentials (board-certified physician for adverse
    medical-necessity determinations).

8. Appealing a PA Denial

A PA denial may be appealed under the Appeal Process Policy. The first
level of appeal must be filed within sixty (60) calendar days of the
denial notice. Expedited appeals are available for urgent care
situations and are decided within seventy-two (72) hours.

9. Exclusions

PA is not a guarantee of payment. Payment is contingent on member
eligibility on the date of service, the service being a covered benefit,
and the absence of claim-level edits (e.g., bundling, modifier rules).

10. Recordkeeping

PA decisions, the underlying clinical criteria version, and the
reviewer's credentials are retained for a minimum of ten (10) years.
