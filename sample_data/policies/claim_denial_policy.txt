Title: Claim Denial Policy
Plan: HealthFirst Cooperative (Demo)
Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Document Owner: Claims Operations

1. Purpose

This policy describes how claim denials are issued, the categories of
denial reasons, member and provider notification requirements, and the
internal review pathway that precedes a formal appeal.

2. Scope

Applies to all medical, behavioral health, and ancillary claims
adjudicated under HealthFirst Cooperative benefit plans.

3. Denial Categories

Claim denials fall into one of the following categories:

  3.1 Administrative Denial
      - Missing or invalid member ID.
      - Service date outside the eligibility period.
      - Provider not credentialed on the date of service.
      - Duplicate of a previously adjudicated claim.

  3.2 Coverage Denial
      - Service is not a covered benefit under the member's plan.
      - Benefit maximum has been reached.
      - Specific exclusion (e.g., cosmetic, experimental, custodial).

  3.3 Medical Necessity Denial
      - The reviewed clinical record does not meet the criteria for the
        requested service.

  3.4 Authorization Denial
      - Prior authorization was required but was not obtained.
      - Service performed exceeds the scope of an issued authorization.

  3.5 Coordination of Benefits (COB) Denial
      - Another payer is primary; the primary explanation of benefits is
        required before HealthFirst will adjudicate.

4. Denial Reason Codes

Each denial includes a HealthFirst denial code in the form HF-XXX:
  - HF-001  Missing/invalid member ID.
  - HF-014  Service not a covered benefit.
  - HF-022  Prior authorization not on file.
  - HF-031  Medical necessity criteria not met.
  - HF-045  Primary payer EOB required.
  - HF-058  Duplicate claim.
  - HF-072  Provider not credentialed on date of service.

5. Member Notification

For every denial that creates a member financial responsibility, an
Explanation of Benefits (EOB) is mailed to the member's address on file
within fifteen (15) calendar days of the adjudication date. The EOB
contains:
  - Service date, provider name, and procedure description.
  - Submitted, allowed, and member-responsibility amounts.
  - Denial code, plain-language reason, and the policy section cited.
  - Instructions to request an internal review or file an appeal.

6. Provider Notification

Providers receive an electronic Remittance Advice (X12N 835) within five
(5) business days of adjudication. The 835 contains Claim Adjustment
Reason Codes (CARC) and Remittance Advice Remark Codes (RARC) mapped
one-to-one to the HealthFirst HF-XXX codes.

7. Internal Review

A provider may request internal review of an administrative or COB
denial without filing a formal appeal. The internal review request must
be submitted within thirty (30) calendar days of the Remittance Advice
date and may be submitted through the Provider Portal or by phone.

Internal review is limited to:
  - Correcting demographic, coding, or COB errors.
  - Resubmitting claims with attached documentation.

Internal review is not available for medical necessity denials — those
must be appealed under the Appeal Process Policy.

8. Member Right to Appeal

For every denial, the member retains the right to file a formal appeal
under the Appeal Process Policy. The appeal must be filed within sixty
(60) calendar days of the denial notice date.

9. Documentation

All denial determinations, the policy section cited, the adjudicator's
identifier, and the date of decision are retained in the claim record
for ten (10) years.
