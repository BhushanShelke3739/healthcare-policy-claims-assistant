Title: Telehealth Billing Policy
Plan: HealthFirst Cooperative (Demo)
Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Document Owner: Provider Reimbursement

1. Purpose

This policy describes how telehealth services are reimbursed under
HealthFirst Cooperative plans, the eligible service categories, the
required claim modifiers and place-of-service (POS) codes, documentation
expectations, and the rules for audio-only encounters.

2. Definitions

  2.1 Telehealth — the use of synchronous, two-way, real-time
      interactive audio-visual technology to deliver health care from a
      distant site provider to a member at an originating site.
  2.2 Audio-Only Telehealth — a synchronous interactive encounter
      conducted via telephone only, without a video component.
  2.3 Store-and-Forward — the asynchronous transmission of clinical
      data (e.g., images) from an originating site to a distant site
      for later review.

3. Eligible Services

The following service categories are eligible for reimbursement when
furnished via telehealth, subject to medical necessity:

  3.1 Office and other outpatient evaluation and management (E/M) visits.
  3.2 Behavioral health services, including psychotherapy and
      medication management.
  3.3 Chronic disease management visits.
  3.4 Speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, and physical
      therapy services for assessment and re-evaluation only.
  3.5 Nutrition counseling and diabetes self-management training.
  3.6 Lactation counseling.

Procedures requiring physical examination, in-person specimen
collection, or hands-on intervention are not eligible.

4. Required Modifiers

Telehealth claims must include one of the following modifiers:
  - 95   Synchronous telehealth via real-time interactive
         audio-and-video.
  - 93   Audio-only telehealth.
  - GT   Synchronous telehealth (legacy; accepted on institutional
         claims).
  - FQ   Audio-only telehealth (behavioral health institutional claims).

A telehealth claim without an appropriate modifier is denied with
HF-067 ("Modifier required for telehealth").

5. Place-of-Service Codes

The following POS codes are accepted on telehealth professional claims:
  - 02   Telehealth — patient not in their home.
  - 10   Telehealth — patient in their home.

Where POS 02 or 10 is used, the modifier requirement in Section 4
still applies.

6. Audio-Only Telehealth

Audio-only telehealth is reimbursed only when:
  6.1 The service falls within an audio-only eligible category (see the
      published Audio-Only List), or
  6.2 The member declined or could not access a video-capable device,
      AND
  6.3 The provider documents in the chart the reason the encounter was
      audio-only.

Audio-only behavioral health services are reimbursed at parity with
audio-visual telehealth.

7. Reimbursement Parity

Eligible telehealth services are reimbursed at the same rate as the
in-person equivalent of the same CPT/HCPCS code, subject to the
applicable contractual fee schedule.

8. Member Cost-Sharing

Telehealth services are subject to the same cost-sharing as the
in-person equivalent under the member's benefit plan. Cost-sharing for
preventive services delivered via telehealth is waived where the
in-person preventive service is provided at no cost.

9. Documentation Requirements

The medical record must contain:
  - The originating site location.
  - The distant site location.
  - The modality (audio-visual, audio-only).
  - The clinical reason for using telehealth, when an in-person visit
    would be the default standard of care.
  - The member's verbal or written consent to receive telehealth
    services.

10. Cross-State Licensure

Distant-site providers must be licensed in the state in which the
member is physically located at the time of service. Claims from
providers without appropriate licensure are denied with HF-074
("Provider not licensed in member's state of service").

11. Store-and-Forward

Store-and-forward services are not reimbursed under this policy except
for specific dermatology and radiology consultations listed on the
Store-and-Forward Eligible List.

12. Audit

Telehealth claims are subject to post-payment audit. Records must be
retained for ten (10) years and produced within thirty (30) calendar
days of a documentation request.
